Finance Glossary

Bull Market A market where prices are rising or expected to rise. Investors feel optimistic.
Bear Market A market where prices fall 20%+ from recent highs. Pessimism dominates.
Market Cap Total value of all shares/coins. Price × Total Supply = Market Cap.
Volatility How much an asset's price fluctuates. High volatility = high risk & reward.
Portfolio Collection of financial investments — stocks, crypto, bonds, cash etc.
Liquidity How quickly an asset can be converted to cash without affecting its price.

Finance Facts for CS Students

01

Bitcoin's source code is open source — any developer can read, fork or contribute to it on GitHub.

02

High-frequency trading firms use algorithms that execute thousands of trades per second — pure CS.

03

Blockchain is essentially a distributed linked list — a data structure you learn in DSA.

04

Google's IPO in 2004 raised $1.67 billion. Their search algorithm is what made it possible.

05

The 2010 Flash Crash wiped $1 trillion in market value in minutes — caused by a trading algorithm bug.
